A "successful" football play, as defined by Football Outsiders, is basically when a play gets enough yardage to keep the offense on track. That is:  50% of needed yardage on 1st down, 70% on 2nd down, and 100% on 3rd or 4th. 

A "big play" (aka an "explosive play") has different definitions for different analysts, but I'm using a generous one:  a run of 12+ yards, or a pass of 16+ yards, is a big play.
